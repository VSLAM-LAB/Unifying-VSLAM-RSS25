Overview

Are Robotics, particularly Visual SLAM, Tackling the Right Challenges? While fields like image segmentation or reinforcement learning have thrived by using vast amounts of unstructured data, visual SLAM is still constrained by a few curated benchmarks. This focus limits the development of robust, scalable systems capable of functioning in complex real-world environments.

Fragmentation across datasets, pipelines, and evaluation metrics is the core issue. Each dataset has its unique structure, making reproducibility and benchmarking difficult. Researchers often waste time adapting to different datasets instead of advancing the field. Additionally, baseline systems are hard to compile due to a lack of standardized pipelines, with dataset-specific issues and failures often undocumented. This fragmentation slows progress significantly.

To advance SLAM, unified approaches across datasets, pipelines, and metrics are needed. Standardizing dataset formats, creating common pipelines for baselines, and expanding beyond traditional metrics like ATE and RPE will allow SLAM to scale and be benchmarked across real-world scenarios.

Workshop Goal: This workshop seeks to bring researchers together to discuss best practices, standardize datasets and pipelines, and streamline SLAM development. By reducing the implementation burden, improving reproducibility, and fostering collaboration, the workshop aims to advance SLAM and unlock its potential to handle large-scale data.

Invited Topics

We invite contributions from the areas of:

  • Standardization of SLAM Datasets
  • Benchmarking Tools and Platforms
  • Streamlining Baseline SLAM Pipelines
  • Reproducibility in SLAM
  • New Evaluation Metrics for SLAM

Review Criteria

Novel submission will be evaluated on:

  • Relevance to the topics of the workshop
  • Recency and novelty
  • Clarity of presentation
  • Technical Quality
  • Strength of results (i.e., the results show promise for early stage work, the (planned) experimental setup is adequate)

Papers that have previously been published will mainly be evaluated on the first two points (relevance and recency). If so, please mention in the submission where it has been published.

Call for Demonstrations

We invite live demonstrations during the poster session. These can either accompany a submitted paper or be standalone. For standalone demonstrations, please submit an up to two pages description and/or video of the demo. Demos will be evaluated on recency and relevance to the workshop.

Provocative Questions We Hope to Discuss

  • How can we establish standardized dataset formats and pipelines to streamline SLAM development and benchmarking across different platforms and research groups?
  • What new evaluation metrics should be introduced to better assess SLAM performance in terms of uncertainty, safety, reliability, and scalability in real-world applications?
  • Is the current reliance on small, curated datasets limiting SLAM's potential, and what strategies can be employed to effectively leverage large-scale, unstructured data in the same way fields like reinforcement learning and computer vision have done?
