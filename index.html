From Fragmented Datasets to Scalable, Real-World Solutions

Overview

Are Robotics, particularly Visual SLAM, Tackling the Right Challenges? While fields like computer vision and reinforcement learning have advanced by harnessing vast amounts of unstructured data, SLAM remains trapped in a small set of carefully curated benchmarks. This narrow focus is limiting the development of robust and scalable systems that can reliably operate “in the wild”, i.e., in complex, real-world environments. The root of the issue lies in the fragmentation across SLAM datasets, pipelines, implementations, and evaluation metrics. Each dataset has its own distinct structure—differing in ground-truth formats, data configurations, and calibration methods and models—making benchmarking and reproducibility a significant challenge. Instead of advancing core technologies, researchers are often forced to spend valuable time adapting their developments to different datasets, stifling innovation. In addition, baseline systems are notoriously difficult to compile and integrate, largely due to the absence of standardized pipelines. Dataset-specific issues and implementation failures often go undocumented, leaving the research community to repeatedly address the same problems in isolation. This lack of cohesion is undoubtedly slowing progress in a field that would greatly benefit from streamlined, unified practices. To drive SLAM forward, we must adopt a unified approach across datasets, pipelines, and evaluation metrics. Standardizing dataset formats, establishing common pipelines for baseline integration, and expanding beyond traditional metrics like Absolute Trajectory Error (ATE) and Relative Pose Error (RPE) will enable SLAM to scale and be benchmarked across a wider range of real-world scenarios, including critical factors like uncertainty, safety, and reliability. Only by addressing this fragmentation can the SLAM community shift its focus to solving larger, more impactful challenges and truly leverage the power of large-scale data to achieve the next breakthroughs.
